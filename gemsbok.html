GEMSBOK

The gemsbok or genbuck (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the genus Oryx. It is endemic to the dry and arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities formaly classified as a subspecies.

The gemsbok is depicted-on the coat of arms of Namibia, where the Oryx gazella species is estimated at 373, 000 indivduals.

The name "gemsbok" in English is derived from Afrikaans gemsbok from the Dutch name of the male chamois, gemsbok. Although some superficial similarities in apperance (especially in the facial pattern) are noticed, the chamois and the oryx are not closely related.
